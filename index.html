Parametric Equations

An amazing (and often beautiful) variety of unique 3-dimensional patterns can be discovered by graphing the following parametric equation:

x = cos(At) + sin(Bt) y = cos(Ct) + sin(Dt) z = cos(Et) + sin(Ft)

where A, B, C, D, E, and F are arbitrary constants, and t increments upward (theoretically towards infinity).

It's amazing how minute adjustments to these constants (sometimes differing by only by 0.001) can produce wildly different results.

Below you can find a live, editable demo as well as a gallery of interesting combinations I've found. If you come up with your own interesting pattern, I'd love to see it!

Interpolating Strange Attractors

The term "strange attractor" is sometimes used in mathematics to describe a chaotic system that seems to be attracted to an ideal behavior. In this project, I was interested in what would happen if I combined multiple chaotic particle systems together. In the demo, you can give a "weight" to each system by increasing or decreasing its slider. Check out the live demo and see what interesting patterns you can create. Use WASD keys or drag/touch to rotate. Scroll mousewheel to zoom in and out.
